The device is powered and ready to administer a dose. A dose can be administered by pressing the dose button on the top of the device two times in a window of 200 ms and 3 seconds between button presses. (80) If the unit reaches the 24-hour limit, it will enter EOU. (A) If the unit fails self-test or has low battery, it will enter EOL.

This is an initiation of a dose of fentanyl. The dosing period is 10 minutes long and delivers 40 mcg of medicine to the patient. During this period, the dose button is disabled ("locked out"), the LED flashes green, and the display indicates that it is in a dosing mode due to the 'walking' LCD animation alternating with the current doses administered. (P) If the unit senses poor skin contact, it will stop dosing, enter an error mode and return to standby. (A) If the unit senses an ALERT mode (compromised skin), it will stop dosing, enter into EOU and no longer be usable.

When a dose is initiated BUT the device does not detect a proper contact with the patient's skin after 4 minutes, the unit will stop dosing and inform the patient of this error. This is indicated by an audible 2-beep pattern along with a flashing red LED. The dose count will not increment, and the device can re-administer a dose after it returns to Ready mode after 1 minute and once skin contact is properly made.

After 80 doses are administered, or 24 hours pass since activation, the device will enter EOU (End of Use) mode. The device will no longer administer any doses, the LCD will flash the total number of doses administered, and the LED does not flash. (A) After 12 hours in EOU, the device will enter EOL mode automatically.

If the device reaches the end of its life cycle, or an ALERT condition is reached (compromised skin), it will flash the current dose amount on the LCD (17 doses in this case), the LED will contintually blink red and an audible 4-beep pattern will play. This indicates that the device has reached EOL (end-of-life). (i/o) Holding down the dose button for 6 seconds will turn off the unit.

SYSTEM SPECIFICATIONS
Active Ingredient Fentanyl HCl
Absorption route: Transdermal (iontophoresis*)
Activation: Patient double-clicks on-demand dosing button (twice within 3 seconds)
Dosing: Pre-programmed 40 micrograms delivered over 10-minute dosing period
Maximum dose rate: 6 doses per hour
Lockout period: 10 minutes (coincides with dosing period)
Doses available per unit: 80
Dispensing life: 24 hours (from initial dose) or 80 doses, whichever comes first

CONTRAINDICATIONS
Patients with allergies to:
  • Fentanyl
  • Cetylpyridinium chloride (an anticeptic in Cepacol mouthwash)**
  • Any component of the IONSYS system

FEATURES
  • On-demand dosing button
  • Red light-emitting diode (LED) that indicates:
    • When a dose is being delivered
    • The approximate number of doses administered
  • Audio tones that signal dose activation and various system alerts
  • Pressure-sensitive adhesive that holds IONSYS securely to patient's skin
  • Built-in safety features:
    • IONSYS is pre-programmed to give a precise dose of pain medication
    • IONSYS is pre-programmed to prevent the patient from getting more than one dose at a time. The patient is limited to a maximum of 6 doses per hour
    • The recessed on-demand dosing button and the "double-click" needed to start a dose protext against accidentally starting a dose
  • IONSYS contains no latex
