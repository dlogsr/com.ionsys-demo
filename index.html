The device is powered and ready to administer a dose. A dose can be administered by pressing the dose button on the top of the device two times in a window of 200 ms and 3 seconds between button presses.

This is an initiation of the first dose of fentanyl. The dosing period is 10 minutes long and delivers 40 mcg of medicine to the patient. During this period, the dose button is disabled ("locked out"), the LED flashes green, and the display indicates that it is in a dosing mode due to the 'walking' LCD animation alternating with the current doses administered.

This is an initiation of a second dose of fentanyl. The dosing period is 10 minutes long and delivers 40 mcg of medicine to the patient. During this period, the dose button is disabled ("locked out"), the LED flashes green, and the display indicates that it is in a dosing mode due to the 'walking' LCD animation alternating with the current doses administered.

When a dose is initiated BUT the device does not detect a proper contact with the patient's skin after 4 minutes, the unit will enter standby mode. This is indicated by an audible 2-beep pattern along with a flashing red LED. The dose count will not increment, and the device can re-administer a dose after it returns to Ready mode and once skin contact is properly made.

When a dose is initiated BUT the device does not detect a proper contact with the patient's skin after 4 minutes, the unit will enter standby mode. This is indicated by an audible 2-beep pattern along with a flashing red LED. The dose count will not increment, and the device can re-administer a dose after it returns to Ready mode and once skin contact is properly made.

After 80 doses are administered, the device has reached EOU (end-of-use) mode. The device will no longer administer any doses, the LCD will flash "80" to indicate it has reached 80 doses, and the LED does not flash.

If the device reaches the end of its life cycle, it will flash the current dose amount on the LCD (17 doses in this case), the LED will contintually blink red and an audible 4-beep pattern will play. This indicates that the device has reached EOL (end-of-life).
